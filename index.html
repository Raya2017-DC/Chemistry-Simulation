Ion exchange is a process where charged particles in a liquid are swapped with charged particles attached to a solid material called a resin. In this simulation, the column is filled with resin beads that start holding one type of ion. As a solution flows through containing a different “target” ion, the resin gradually replaces its original ions with the target ions. This creates a moving “exchange front” where the swapping is actively occurring. At first, the outlet stays clean because the resin is capturing the target ions, but over time the resin becomes full. Once the exchange front reaches the bottom of the column, target ions begin appearing in the outlet — this is called breakthrough. The simulation shows how flow rate, capacity, and selectivity affect how quickly the resin becomes exhausted.
